Craft & execution

Pixel-perfect attention to detail, understanding of visual hierarchy, typography, motion, and interaction patterns

Project: Optavia Design System

Problem

The brand felt dated and inconsistent. Darby Sans was used everywhere, creating readability issues. Twelve different gradients gave the product an “earthy and dirty” appearance. Every component felt generic, with no systematic approach to typography, color, or spacing.

Approach

We audited the entire product, cataloging inconsistencies across components and patterns. Working with developers, we studied how they built features and designed a system they’d actually adopt. We referenced Dropbox and Shopify’s systems to understand scalable approaches.

Solution

We established a refined typographic hierarchy using Brando (modern serif for headers with tight tracking) and Gibson (open-source sans serif for body text with generous line height). We created a simplified, accessibility-compliant color palette that modernized the brand while keeping migration costs low. An 8px spacing system and defined component states ensured consistency across all touchpoints.

Impact

Coaches and clients immediately adopted the new brand, creating their own materials using the updated design language. Users described it as more professional and trustworthy.
