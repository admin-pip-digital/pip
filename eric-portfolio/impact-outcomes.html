Impact & outcomes

Tying design decisions to business metrics and user value, not just aesthetic choices

Project: Optavia Homepage Redesign

Problem

A wellness company needed a landing page that could both educate prospects and drive conversions. They were testing our internal approach against an external agency's work to determine which would perform better.

Approach

The external agency went heavy on brand styling and visual flourishes. We took a different approach - focusing on user needs over aesthetics, clear navigation, straightforward journey mapping, and reducing friction in the conversion flow. The constraint of working within existing infrastructure forced us to prioritize what actually mattered.

Solution

We created a simplified user flow that matched how people actually wanted to engage: clear entry points (login, start journey, find coach), streamlined information architecture, mobile-first responsive design, and faster load times through cleaner code.

Impact

Head-to-head testing over one month showed our constrained approach outperformed the agency's: 22% higher conversion rate, 7% better engagement, and 6% lower bounce rate. Sometimes the best design is the one users don't have to think about - constraints forced us to focus on removing barriers rather than looking impressive.
