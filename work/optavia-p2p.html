From Coach-Dependent to Client-Led: Designing a Strategic E-Commerce Pivot

Context

User research was done by the Director of UX (my boss) to investigate a decline in coach performance. I assisted with interviewing some of the 20 coaches, then he analyzed results and compiled recommendations that were presented to leadership and used to make a strategic decision.

The Problem

Optavia's coaches had saturated their personal networks. They'd successfully grown their client bases by selling to friends, family, and connections - but there was nowhere left to expand. The business model that got them this far couldn't scale further. User research led by our UX director revealed the core issue: coaches needed access to clients beyond their existing networks, and potential clients existed who wanted the program but had no coach connection.

The Strategic Shift

Leadership decided to pivot from coach-dependent acquisition to company-led marketing. Instead of relying solely on coaches to find clients, Optavia would use marketing and a set of new user pathways to connect strangers with coaches and allow clients to self-initiate their health journey. This was fundamental restructuring - we were building a new front door to the business.

Approach

I owned the entire client experience for this new acquisition model across web, marketing, e-commerce, and the mobile app. The central design challenge was creating a "goal-to-plan" questionnaire system that would:

  • Help clients understand which program fit their needs
  • Match them with appropriate coaches
  • Enable self-service purchasing while preserving the coach relationship
  • Scale beyond what individual coach networks could reach

The Solution

Working collaboratively with the marketing, nutrition, legal, and development teams, I designed:

  • Use case mapping for different client types (weight loss, GLP-1 support, maintenance, athletic performance)
  • User pathways from initial awareness through coach connection and first purchase
  • Questionnaire logic that educated users while gathering data for appropriate routing
  • Technical strategy to support coach matching and program recommendations
  • Systematic solutions that could adapt as new programs and user needs emerged

The Execution Reality

This happened during significant industry disruption (GLP-1 medications changing the weight loss landscape). The complexity wasn't just the design - it was aligning cross-functional teams around a fundamental business model change while market conditions were shifting under us. We built and launched despite the uncertainty.

The Impact

By designing pathways for clients to find coaches independently rather than just improving existing coach tools, we unlocked markets that individual networks could never reach. This wasn't just UX work - it was business model design. The systematic approach enabled Optavia to adapt as both user needs and unpredictable market conditions (GLP-1 disruption) continued to evolve.
